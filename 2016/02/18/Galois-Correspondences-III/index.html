Galois Correspondences III

Posted on 2016-02-18 Edited on 2022-10-01 In Math

This time, we consider another kind of Galois correspondence: the one for covering spaces. This is actually somehow trivial.

Preliminaries

Let XXX be a topological space. We first recall some notions in topology.

Definition: A covering space YYY over XXX is a bundle p ⁣:Y→Xp\colon Y\to Xp:Y→X which is locally trival and with discrete fibers. In plain words, the condition means for every point x∈Xx\in Xx∈X, there exists an open neighborhood UUU such that the pullback p−1(U)→Up^{-1}(U)\to Up−1(U)→U is isomorphic to a projection U×F→UU\times F\to UU×F→U with FFF a nonempty discrete set.

\begin{gather*} p^{-1}(U) \stackrel{\cong}{\longrightarrow} U\times F \\ p\searrow\quad\swarrow\mathrm{pr} \\ U \end{gather*}

Definition: The fundamental group π(X,x)\pi(X,x)π(X,x) of XXX at a point x∈Xx\in Xx∈X is the group of homotopy classes of loops through xxx and lie in XXX.

Under the assumption that XXX is connected, locally path-connected and semi-locally simply-connected, the group π(X,x)\pi(X,x)π(X,x) is independent of the choice of xxx. In this case, we denote π(X)\pi(X)π(X) for short. However, we still need to fix a base point xxx. We will keep this assumption throughout this post.

The universal covering space

Let X~\widetilde{X}X be the topological space whose underlying set is the set of all homotopy classes of paths in XXX starting at the base point xxx and whose topology is the weakest one making the following map continuous:

p ⁣:X~→X,[γ]↦γ(1).p\colon\widetilde{X}\to X,\quad [\gamma]\mapsto\gamma(1). p:X→X,[γ]↦γ(1).

For convenience, we fix the bas point x~\tilde{x}x~ of X~\widetilde{X}X to be the homotopy class of the trivial loops through xxx.

Then p ⁣:X~→Xp\colon\widetilde{X}\to Xp:X→X is the universal covering space of XXX in the sense that for any connected covering space Y→XY\to XY→X, there exists a morphism of covering spaces of XXX:

\begin{gather*} \widetilde{X} \stackrel{\exists!}{\longrightarrow} Y \\ \searrow\quad\swarrow \\ X \end{gather*}

and this morphism is unique if we require it to preserve the base points. When we choice y∈p−1(x)y\in p^{-1}(x)y∈p−1(x) to be the base point of YYY, the morphism is given as follows: the unique path-lifting lemma allows us to lift every path γ\gammaγ start at xxx in XXX to a path start at yyy in YYY, we define the image of [γ][\gamma][γ] to be the end of this path.

Note that the universal covering space naturally has a free π(X)\pi(X)π(X)-action given by composition of homotopy classes of paths. For any subgroup HHH of π(X)\pi(X)π(X), the orbit space X~/H\widetilde{X}/HX/H also gives a connected covering space of XXX whose fibers are isomorphic to π(X)/H\pi(X)/Hπ(X)/H.

Now, we get the Galois correspondence for covering spaces.

Theorem: Let XXX be a connected, locally path-connected and semi-locally simply-connected space. Then we have an equivalence of categories

Cov/X≅π(X)-Set.\mathrm{Cov}/X\cong\pi(X)\text{-}\mathrm{Set}. Cov/X≅π(X)-Set.

Rethinking

It is easy to verify that the universal covering space X~\widetilde{X}X represents the functor Fx\mathcal{F}_xFx​. Moreover, we have $$\mathrm{Aut}_X(\widetilde{X}) \cong \pi(X),$$ and $$\mathrm{Aut}_X(\widetilde{X}) < \mathrm{Aut}(\mathcal{F}_x).$$

However, AutX(X~)\mathrm{Aut}_X(\widetilde{X})AutX​(X) is in general not isomorphic to Aut(Fx)\mathrm{Aut}(\mathcal{F}_x)Aut(Fx​).

What’s more, by only assume XXX is connected, the functor Fx\mathcal{F}_xFx​ still works and is independent of the choice of xxx, while the universal covering X~\widetilde{X}X may not exists.
